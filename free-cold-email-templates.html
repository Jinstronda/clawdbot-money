üìà Cold Email Best Practices for 2026

1. Keep It Short

The ideal cold email is 50-125 words. Every sentence should earn its place. If you can delete a sentence without losing meaning, delete it.

2. Personalize Beyond {First Name}

Reference something specific: their recent post, a product launch, a shared connection. Personalized cold emails get 2-3x higher response rates than generic templates.

3. One CTA Per Email

Don't ask them to check your website AND book a call AND watch a demo. One clear ask. Make it easy to say yes.

4. Subject Lines Matter Most

47% of recipients decide to open an email based on the subject line alone. Keep them under 7 words, personal, and curiosity-inducing. Our HookSmith headline generator can help brainstorm subject lines too.

5. Follow Up 3-5 Times

80% of deals require at least 5 follow-ups. Space them 3-5 business days apart. Each follow-up should add new value ‚Äî don't just "bump" the thread.

6. Check Readability Before Sending

Your cold email should be readable at a 5th-8th grade level. Use our free readability scorer to check before hitting send.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

What is a cold email?+
A cold email is an unsolicited email sent to a potential client, partner, or contact with no prior relationship. Unlike spam, cold emails are personalized, targeted, and offer genuine value to the recipient.
What is a good cold email response rate?+
A good cold email response rate is 5-15%. Top performers achieve 20-30% by using highly personalized templates, strong subject lines, and strategic follow-ups. The average across industries is around 1-5%.
How many follow-ups should I send?+
Research shows 3-5 follow-ups is optimal. 80% of sales require 5 follow-ups, but 44% of salespeople give up after just one. Space them 3-5 business days apart, and always add new value in each follow-up.
Is cold emailing legal?+
Cold emailing is legal in most countries when done properly. In the US, the CAN-SPAM Act requires a valid physical address and unsubscribe option. In the EU, GDPR requires a legitimate interest basis. Always research local regulations and include an opt-out option.
